You can use the Excel version or the plain-text if you want to be able to move the rows and columns around; either way, to see the phonetic symbols properly, look at the PDF version, or download the free font SILDoulosIPA93.

Phonetics

This list is condensed from Karen Chung's post to the Linguist List. Click here to see the original list. 

The Internation Phonetic Association
IPA charts, fonts, audio clips.

A Course in Phonetics: Vowels and Consonants
(Peter Ladefoged, UCLA)
Web supplements for A Course in Phonetics. Click on symbols in an IPA chart and hear them pronounced! Lots more, including interesting sounds from real languages.

Speech organs
(John Coleman, Oxford)
Great graphics. Includes downloadable QuickTime movie of x-ray speech). pictures of larynx, diagram of motion of vocal cords.

X-Ray film database for speech research
(Kevin Munhall, Queen's; Eric Vatikiotis-Bateson and Yoh'ichi Tohkura, ATR Human Information Processing Laboratories)
Amazing x-ray movies of people talking. Includes a sample of Canadian French!

Sound waves
(Kevin Russell, Manitoba)
Intro to speech waves and spectrograms
(Marcus Filipsson, Lund)
Basic acoustics.

The vOICe Sonification Applet Spectrogram version of The vOICe Sonification Applet
(Peter Meijer)
Crazy and fun. Convert graphics to sound and vice-versa.

Center for Spoken Language Understanding
Several tutorials, including spectrograms.

The Human Ear
(Andrew Marsh, University of Western Australia)
Basics of audition.

Online phonetics course
(Christophe Pythoud, Université de Lausanne)
SIPhTrA voicing basics tutorial
(University College London)
SIPhTrA Plosives 1: basics
SIPhTrA Plosives 2: Voicing onset time and aspiration
Tutorials.

Sound machines
(John Maidment)
Learn to reconize sounds.
