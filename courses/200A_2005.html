Keley-i (due Oct. 25)

From K&K textbook, pp. 329-330. Some tips/remarks:

  • Please do formulate the gemination and reduplication rules that apply only to the future forms. You may use transformational notation for them, and just add the note that they apply only to future forms. The reason I ask you to formulate the rules explicitly is that you should consider their order with respect to each other and other rules.
  • But, when it comes to the rules that you see applying with the [me...-] and [ne...-] prefixes (the "..." is because it's up to you to decide the underlying forms), please don't use transformational notation. I ask this because transformational notation makes it possible to collapse two rules whose ordering must otherwise be thought about.
  • The focus of this assignment is rule ordering--is there a single, linear order? are some rules left unordered and thus subject to Anderson's theory of natural ordering? I don't think that cyclicity or Lexical Phonology come into it.

Fijian (due Dec. 8)

Formulate an OT analysis of the Fijian data discusses in class on Nov. 29 (MS Word version).
Directions:

  • Use feet--they will make things easier.
  • Account for why stress falls where it does.
  • Account for vowel shortening, but assume there is no lengthening--that is, assume that all long vowels are underlyingly long.
  • Account for diphthong shortening, but only in three of the four dialects shown-- skip the second one (where [aw] shortens to [o] and [aj] shortens to [e]).
  • You can use constraints from the reading, like ONSET (p. 17), PARSE-C and PARSE-V (p. 25, or MAX-C and MAX-V, if you prefer), EDGEMOST (p. 29; see also p. 39), PKPROM (p. 39), FTBIN (p. 50), RHTYPE=T (p. 56), WEIGHT-TO-STRESS PRINCIPLE (p. 56), PARSE-SYLL (p. 62-- note that this penalizes syllables that are not included in a foot; it has nothing to do with deletion, despite the name), RHHRM (p. 63).
  • You may also want a constraint PARSE-MORA (or MAX-MORA, if you prefer), which penalizes deletion of a mora. And you may want to invent a constraint that forbids unfooted material between two feet--be sure to specify how violations are to be counted.
  • Make sure the candidates in your tableaux have enough information shown to evaluate the constraints. For example, if ONSET plays a role in your tableau, be sure to include syllable boundaries in the candidates. Draw in all foot boundaries in all candidates. If, say, mora deletion occurs in a tableau, be sure to draw in associated moras for the input and for all the candidates. Don't forget to include the input (underlying form) in every tableau.
